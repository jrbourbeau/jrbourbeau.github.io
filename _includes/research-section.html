Research

Cosmic-ray Physics

My research is focused on studying the cosmic-ray (CR) composition spectrum using the IceCube South Pole Neutrino Observatory. The central task is to produce an algorithm that can accurately classify different particles (e.g. protons, helium nuclei, iron nuclei, etc). More information can be found on the GitHub page for this analysis.

High Energy Pheno

During my first two years of graduate school I worked with Yang Bai in the high energy phenomenology group on investigating a specific dark matter signature produced by Z' jets at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We, along with our collaborator Tongyan Lin, published a paper on this topic in the Journal for High Energy Physics [arXiv:1504.01395].

Y. Bai, J. Bourbeau, and T. Lin. "Dark matter searches with a mono-Z' jet," JHEP 06, 205 (2015). DOI: 10.1007/JHEP06 (2015) 205.
