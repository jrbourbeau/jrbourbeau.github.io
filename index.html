Research

Cosmic-ray Composition

My current work focuses on applying machine learning techniques to data collected using the IceCube South Pole Neutrino Observatory. The goal of my analysis is to study the composition dependence of the cosmic-ray (CR) flux and arrival-direction anisotropy, which can help guide us towards answering fundamental questions about the origin of cosmic rays. More information can be found on the GitHub page for this analysis.

Dark Matter Phenomenology

During my first two years of graduate school I worked with Yang Bai in the high energy phenomenology group on investigating a specific dark matter signature produced by Z' jets at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We, along with our collaborator Tongyan Lin, published a paper on this topic in the Journal for High Energy Physics [arXiv:1504.01395].

Y. Bai, J. Bourbeau, and T. Lin. "Dark matter searches with a mono-Z' jet," JHEP 06, 205 (2015). DOI: 10.1007/JHEP06 (2015) 205.
