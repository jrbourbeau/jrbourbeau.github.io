About me

Currently, I'm a physics PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My research centers around studying cosmic-rays at the IceCube Neutrino Observatory. Specifically, I'm studying the cosmic-ray arrival direction anisotropy.

I received my bachelor's degree in physics from the University of Texas at Arlington in 2013. While at UTA I worked with Andrew Brandt on developing a high precision time-of-flight detector as part of a potential upgrade to the ATLAS detector at the LHC. My work primarily focused on improving and maintaining the data analysis code base of the group.

In my free time I like being outside with friends and enjoying life in MadTown with my MadCats!

Research

Cosmic-ray Physics

I am a member of the IceCube collaboration, working with Stefan Westerhoff and the cosmic-ray working group. My research focuses on using data collected by the IceCube and IceTop detectors to study cosmic rays. Currently, I'm studying the cosmic-ray arrival direction anisotropy and investigating a new reconstruction method for IceTop events.

High Energy Pheno

During my first two years of graduate school I worked with Yang Bai in the high energy phenomenology group on investigating a specific dark matter signature produced by Z' jets at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We, along with our collaborator Tongyan Lin, published a paper on this topic in the Journal for High Energy Physics [arXiv:1504.01395].

  • Y. Bai, J. Bourbeau, and T. Lin. "Dark matter searches with a mono-Z' jet," JHEP 06, 205 (2015). DOI: 10.1007/JHEP06 (2015) 205.
