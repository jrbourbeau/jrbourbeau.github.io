About me

Hi, I'm James Bourbeau. I'm a physics Ph.D. student and data analyst with an interest in scientific computing, machine learning, and software development. I have several years experience programming, predominantly in the Python data science space. In my free time I enjoy spending time outside with friends, cross-country skiing, and enjoying life in Madison, WI!

Currently, I'm working as a member of the IceCube South Pole Neutrino Observatory at the University of Wisconsin—Madison. My work focuses primarily on applying machine learning and statistical deconvolution techniques to data collected with IceCube to study the cosmic-ray composition spectrum.
